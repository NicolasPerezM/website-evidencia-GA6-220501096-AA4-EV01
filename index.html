Step 2: Collect the insects

Get the right gear

Butterfly net

Use a butterfly net to catch flying insects. Look for one with a long hanlde and soft mesh

A jar with a lid and small holes of air

A clear jar allows you to se the insects inside. Make sure it has small holes to le in air

A pooter (a small, hand-heid device used to collect insects)

A pooter is useful for collecting small insects. It consists of a tube with two chambers and a one-way valve

Catch the insects

Be gentle

hand insects with care to avoid injuring them. Use a soft net and be gentle when transferring them to a jar

Move slowly

Insects are sensitive to movement and may fly away if you approach too quickly. Move slowly and quietly to avoid scaring them off

Watch and wait

Observe the insects from a distance before attempting to catch them . This will give you a better sense of their behavior and make it easier to predict their movements

Keep them safe

Paper towel

Add a piece of damp paper towel to the jar to provide moisture

Holes in the jar lid

Poke small holes in the jar lid to allow air to circulate

Twigs or leaves

Place a few twigs or leaves in the jar to create a natural habitat
